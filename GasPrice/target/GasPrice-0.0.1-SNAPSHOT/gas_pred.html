Applications

This predictor can be utilized in the following domains:

Commercial-

  • Retailers: Online shopping increase dramatically along with gas prices. Retailers can plan promotional offers, sales and shipping logistics based on gas prices.
  • Realtors: Houses in areas with longer commutes tend to sell at lower prices and homes with shorter commutes tend to sell at higher prices when gasoline goes up.
  • Public Transportation: Higher gas prices result in noticeable increases in public transportation ridership. Increase in commuters require planning of transit services and vehicle allocation for busy routes.
  • Work and School Week Planning: Some businesses, including colleges, have elected to go with four-day weeks to limit their employees' or students' financial burden for commuting. For example, in 2008, Southwestern Community College announced a four-day school week to help students and staff save on gas money.
  • New Jobs Hiring: Rising gas prices force some businesses to reevaluate their hiring plans, holding off because they are uncertain about the economy's health. Less spending results in decreased sales, both of which can influence a company's ability to hire.

Civilian-

  • Travel Planning: Gas price prediction can benefit people planning road trips and help in pocket friendly travel.
  • Real Estate Sale: The ideal time to sale real estate can be calculated with respect to the gas prices and benefit a civilian to maximize the profit.
  • Expenditure Planning: In 2015, it was estimated that consumers spent 80 cents of every dollar saved on gas on personal consumption, according to a JPMorgan Chase report. More the gas price, lesser dining out and lesser shopping.
